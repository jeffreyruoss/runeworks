# Runeworks — Design Improvement Ideas

## Early Game Feel

The document jumps straight into complex systems, but the first few minutes of play need to hook people. Consider a "freeplay" opening stage with no timer or star pressure — just let players discover how buildings connect and items flow. The satisfaction of watching your first quarry feed a forge feed a workbench is the moment that sells the game. Don't rush past it.

Also think about giving players a small starting stockpile of stone on every stage. Having to quarry stone just to build your first quarry is a bootstrapping problem that could feel frustrating rather than strategic.

## The Quest System Has More Potential

Right now quests are just "produce X, get gold." They could do more narrative and mechanical work. What if some quests are mutually exclusive — a merchant and a scholar both want your limited crystal shards, and you pick who to serve? That creates meaningful choice beyond optimization.

Timed quests could add spice too. "The caravan leaves in 60 seconds — can you fill the order?" suddenly makes you weigh whether to interrupt your main production line. And chain quests — where completing one unlocks a follow-up with better rewards — would give stages more of a story arc.

## The Grid Needs More Interesting Terrain

Obstacles right now are just blockers. What if terrain had positive effects too? A river tile could boost adjacent forge speed (cooling). Ancient ruins could give free research points if you build an Arcane Study next to them. Elevated terrain could extend Mana Tower range. This turns the grid from a puzzle of avoidance into a puzzle of opportunity — you're not just working around obstacles, you're racing to claim the best positions.

## A "Blueprint" or "Template" System

Once players figure out a good layout for, say, an ingot-to-cogwheel chain, they'll want to reuse it. Let them save small building arrangements as blueprints they can stamp down in future stages. It respects the player's time and rewards mastery. It also makes the grid pressure more interesting because now you're thinking about whether your favorite blueprint even fits on this particular map.

## The Mana Network Feels Underdeveloped

The document mentions Mana Wells, Obelisks, and Towers but doesn't give mana much to do beyond powering the Arcane Study. What if mana was a second "currency" flowing through the grid — buildings in the mana network get passive bonuses like faster processing or larger buffers, and you have to decide how much grid space to dedicate to extending the network versus building more production. That makes mana network layout a genuine strategic layer rather than just a prerequisite checkbox.

## Visibility of What's Happening

Factory games live or die on feedback. A few ideas: show items visually hopping between buildings so you can see your chain working. Color-code buildings by status — green for active, yellow for starved, red for blocked — so bottlenecks are visible at a glance without waiting for the results screen. Add a real-time throughput counter showing items per minute for each building. The bottleneck hints on the results screen are great, but players should be able to read their factory's health while it's running.

## A "Prestige" Layer Across Stages

Let players earn permanent "mastery points" from stars that unlock small passive bonuses (like a 5% stone discount on all buildings, or starting with a free chest). This gives long-term progression without making individual stages trivial. It also gives completionists a reason to go back and 3-star earlier stages.

## Sound and Rhythm

This is a keyboard-only game with ticking simulations — there's a natural rhythm to it. Consider tying audio cues to production events. A subtle chime when an item is crafted, a deeper tone when a quarry depletes, a satisfying cash register sound when interest ticks up. Done well, a running factory becomes a little music box, and you can hear when something goes wrong before you see it.

## Recommendations on Open Questions

- **Gold** should reset per stage — persistent gold across stages would make balancing nearly impossible and punish players who don't grind earlier levels.
- **Building upgrades** should also reset per stage, but unlocked recipes and building types should persist. This keeps each stage a fresh puzzle while still rewarding progression.
- **Starting resources** — give players a small amount of stone (10–15) so the first few seconds are about decisions, not waiting.
- **Sandbox mode** — absolutely build one. Factory game players love to tinker, and it costs very little to implement since the simulation engine already exists.
